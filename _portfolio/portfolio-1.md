New York City's severe traffic congestion is significantly impacted by the daily influx of interstate commuters from New Jersey. How do new tolling schemes and personal policy attitudes reshape these travel choices? We employ multinomial and mixed logit models, incorporating segmentation tests on policy effectiveness beliefs, to understand and predict peak-hour mode shifts from New Jersey to Manhattan. By quantifying commuters' willingness-to-pay and cost sensitivities across different attitude segments, our behavioral modeling provides actionable insights to support equitable policy design and effective urban traffic management."
